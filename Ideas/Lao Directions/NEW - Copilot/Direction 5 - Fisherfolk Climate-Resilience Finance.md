# Direction 5: Fisherfolk Climate-Resilience Finance

Back to Directions: [[Directions Overview|Directions Overview]] | Back to MOC: [[Hackathon MOC]]

This direction is derived from the LGU-CSO directory and focused on climate-vulnerable fisherfolk and coastal livelihoods.

---

### The Unique Angle
**Use existing local organization networks as a finance activation layer.**  
Instead of starting from zero, we start from already listed cooperatives/fisherfolk groups and make them data-ready for faster, fairer financial support.

---

### Concept
Build a partner-facing tool that:
1. Cleans and standardizes organization/member registry data.
2. Overlays climate exposure and livelihood sensitivity.
3. Produces explainable member/group readiness and vulnerability scores.
4. Recommends FSP actions: pre-shock emergency credit, savings protection nudges, and post-shock fast-track support.

---

### Why It's Different
Most solutions propose new apps for end users. This direction focuses on the **execution bottleneck inside FSP/coop operations**: who to prioritize, when to intervene, and which product to deploy based on data quality and risk context.

---

### Risk
Directory presence does not guarantee organizational readiness or active participation.  
Mitigation: staged outreach and a strict qualification checklist before pilot commitment.
